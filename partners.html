"Gelecek Namine" vetendas cemiyyetinin inkisafina destek ictimai birliyi

"Gelecek Namine" is a National Non Governmental Organisation based in Azerbaijan. It was established in 2010 to meet a growing demand for international entrepreneurship youth work services. Through a team of dedicated and experienced coaches and trainers, Our team provides interactive and customised training, coaching and capacity building in entrepreneurial development and leadership skills. Highly skilled and experienced our professionals support young people to become successful entrepreneurs, leaders and positive role models in their local and international communities. They boost their levels of self-esteem, confidence, entrepreneurial, communication and social skills and tailor these skills to match specific context, culture and objectives. "Gelecek Namine" members have previously been actively involved in the Entrepreneurship & Youth sector since 2005 and also have a long track record working with economically and socially deprived communities. "Gelecek Namine" commitment for developing, supporting and promoting volunteering is seen from a strong and loyal volunteer base. Working team as well as volunteers at "Gelecek Namine" have coordinated and partnered in a number of successful EU funded projects with other organisations from across Europe and Asia. Through these projects team and volunteers have developed significant expertise and experience.

Uludag Universitesi

The aim of Uludag University is to bring up contemporary students who identify themselves with the cultural and historical values of the country by being respectable to the national values, have a high personal responsibility, and free thought on the contemporary, secular, and democratic way led by reason and science, and drawn by Ataturk.
Uludag University, established in 1975 under the name of Bursa University and took its current name in 1982, has been successfully continuing its educational services ever since with its 13 faculties, 2 schools, 15 vocational schools, 1 conservatory, 4 institutes, 21 applied research centers and 5 departments under rectorate. The vision of Uludag University is to produce universal knowledge, to contribute to society by educating qualified students, by providing quality service and integrating with the industrial city, Bursa. Also, it aims to be an institution whose members feel proud to be part of.

ASSOTSITSIA DOPOLNITELNOGO OBRAZOVANIYA I PROSVESHTENIYA

The Association of Life-Long Learning and Enlightenment is a non-commercial association, uniting 13 educational organizations and establishments of Belarus. Association`s activity is aimed at coordination of development of the additional and non-formal education and enlightenment in Belarus. The Association of Life-Long Learning and Enlightenment was registered on 4th February, 2011. Its member-organizations have been cooperating in this field for more than 10 years, implementing different educational projects, activities and events applying the non-formal educational approach.
Priorities:
To develop and promote civic education in Belarus;
To support the development of regional organizations of non-formal education in their efforts to implement the Non-Formal Educational approach;
To develop and implement training programmes of managers, trainers, facilitators in the sphere of Non-Formal Education

ASSOCIACAO INTERCULTURAL PARA TODOS

IAFA is a non-profit association with a work team composed of personnel licensed and master's degrees in various fields which allows us to monitor effectively in all its aspects. This is why we have experienced team that deals with people from different countries and cultures and we have all the conditions necessary to embrace the different kinds of projects since we have all the resources needed. IAFA works as a hosting, sending and coordinating organization, which means that we are able not only to participate as partners, but also to implements projects of our own.
Our staff has experience in supporting projects such as Leonardo Da Vinci, Comenius and coordinating events for Erasmus students, among others. This experience was very positive and motivating in terms of doing more for intercultural Europe.
Our association has developed contacts with several companies in the region, in different sectors of activity, which enables us to place, support and monitor the participants of our projects in the active working life. The contacts we have developed with these entities have been very enriching for both parties, as they benefit from knowledge sharing and interaction. These initiatives allow to the participants to be in contact with the work context of a different culture where they can apply the knowledge acquired. These activities especially allow them to develop their entrepreneurial skills and helping them build higher self-esteem and confidence.
We also promote intercultural cooperation between people with different dynamic programs covering topics such as human rights, equal opportunities and promoting awareness of the need for voluntary collective solidarity, tolerance and social inclusion.
The mission of our association is based on four fundamental pillars:

  • To promote interculturalism and multiculturalism among people;
  • Creation, promotion and support of cultural, society and social integration;
  • To support, educate and encourage entrepreneurship and European culture.
  • Contribute through their activities to reduce unemployment and the disclosure of Europe as an opportunity.
